Hello, and welcome to the Toka Language!

This is an experimental language, designed to be easily learned, modified, and extended to fill a variety of roles. The language and implementation is not static, and continues to evolve as needs change.

Toka is similar to Forth, but don't expect your existing knowledge to apply directly to Toka. Toka allows a good deal of low-level control, while supporting some useful abstractions that make development easier. Some influences also come from RetroForth, HelFORTH, 4p, Factor, and SmallTalk.

If you can't find something, try the index. A more structured set of topics can be found in First Steps.
